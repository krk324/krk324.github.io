Abra UX Case Study

By: Kaga Kawabata
Date: 2018/07/15
Read time: 10 mins

Overview

Cryptocurrency/Blockchain are the next promising technology with a potential to innovate how we transfer a value just as the internet enables people to transfer information instantly. Word blockchain has been a buzzword and many apps that use this new tech have emerged. However, most apps UI/UX designs are unintuitive. While core service development is the highest priority for early-stage cutting-edge technology like blockchain, I believe implementation of design methods will help improve the overall accessibility and service quality which opens up the doors to non-technical users for mass adoption. This article will look into UI/UX of Abra, one of the popular and oldest apps in the States. I hope this article will help contribute to raising the baseline of app/web design in blockchain/cryptocurrency industry.

 

Objectives

To explore the human-centered design process by evaluating the usability of apps. I chose to do a case study on Abra. Abra is cryptocurrency wallet and exchange intent to be the simplest place to invest in cryptocurrency. I am not affiliated with Abra. I am just looking for ways to challenge myself as a designer.

 

Problem & Hypothesis

While working in crypto/blockchain industry for over 3+ years I realized there are many unintuitive apps that are difficult to use. Abra is one of those apps I’ve been following since the inception. As I had difficulties buying coins using the Abra’s app, I assumed other users also have same issues. Through this UX case study, I hope to help pursue Abra’s mission statement “cryptocurrency investing simplified.”

 

User research

 

Proto-Persona

As a starting point, I created a proto-persona based on my own experience I had at cryptocurrency exchange company and by conducting a brief online interview with my friends who have invested in cryptocurrency before.

 

Scenarios

I created the following scenarios and job story to prepare for the usability test.

 

Ryan buys cryptocurrency.
  • 1) Ryan heard about new promising cryptocurrency from his friend he trust.
  • 2) Ryan opens the app and searches and finds the new cryptocurrency. After doing a simple research he decides to buy.
  • 3) Ryan realized he needs to add money to his account, so he adds money.
  • 4) Ryan buys new cryptocurrency

 

Job Story

When my friend I can trust ping me a notice about new promising cryptocurrency, I want to learn about it and buy it, so I can own it and increase my savings when the price goes up.

 

Guerrilla Usability Testing

Over two days I’ve tested 7 strangers at blockchain related events and cafe in San Francisco. Based on my persona, I checked if they qualify as test users. Following is a simple user demographic of our test users.

3/7 Have purchased cryptocurrency before.

5/7 Use apps like acorn and Robinhood to invest.

After validating test users, based on scenarios and job story I conducted a guerrilla usability testing. Before the tests, I asked all the participants to imagine the following scenario.

 

Scenario: Your friend you can trust recommended you to buy Ethereum. You are considering buying Ethereum.

All participants were given the following tasks:

  • How do you check the price of ETH?
  • How will you add new cryptocurrency to the home screen?
  • Let say you decided to buy ETH. If you want to add money to your account how will you do that?
  • Now you have a balance in your account, how will you buy $5 worth of ETH?

    Analysis - Identify and prioritize pain points

     

    Affinity mapping

    I categorized the results of user interviews by pain points using a spreadsheet. Following are the analyzed key results.

    • 7/7 users I tested had difficulties buying a crypto where some user gave up on the app in the middle of the test.
    • 6/7 users had difficulties checking the price of cryptocurrencies.
    • 5/7 users had difficulties going back to the home screen. They couldn’t identify the button portfolio on the right-hand corner that direct them to the home screen.
    • 4/7 had difficulties adding new coins to their portfolio.

     

    2x2 Matrix

    After categorizing the pain points I prioritized them using 2x2 matrix. As a result, I decided to focus on the improvement of features that relates to buying and selling.

    Business Goals: Direct users to buy and sell many cryptocurrencies as possible.
