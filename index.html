I

Raised in Chicago, with roots in Toyama. Waseda School of International Liberal Studies, then DePaul's graduate school. Joined Nomura Research Institute as a new grad; at BEENOS, worked in the CEO's office on venture-capital support and cross-border commerce. Studied programming at General Assembly, then founded a bento delivery service and a dating app. The beginning of a restless life shuttling between 🇺🇸 and 🇯🇵.

In 2016, joined Coincheck as the second hire. Led international business development, marketing, HR, and web. During the ~$500M hack, led international coordination to trace the stolen funds. Left after the acquisition. The illusion of money collapsed in a single night.

Coincheck — Blockchain Industry Group

After the illusion of money fell apart, I wanted to meet people working for both greater success and greater social meaning. Went to Silicon Valley. Studied product design and management at Tradecraft and supported US/Japan startups. I was still inside the structure of "success."

Silicon Valley diary (note)
II

Once the illusion lifted, the search for what I wanted began. I even released music on Spotify. There was no set path anymore. I did everything I'd ever wanted to. But no matter what I did, meaning slipped away.

In the middle of that search, I went to Dhamma Dicchā for a 12-day Vipassana meditation retreat. In total silence, I observed my inner world. From that raw experience, a wish emerged: to deliver consciousness transformation to people. And I saw it — fear and anxiety ("I must succeed to be valued") had been my engine. From now on, I want to move forward in the feeling of love. An hour of meditation every day — still my practice.

My Vipassana retreat (note)

Walking in the feeling of love, I launched Well-being AI products at ExaWizards and experienced its IPO from the inside. Alongside meditation, I started learning coaching as another path to consciousness transformation. Then I noticed: behind the goodwill of solving social problems, my own ego — still searching for meaning — was hiding.

About me (note)

Looking for more ways to invite consciousness transformation in people, I entered Titan's school and performed as comedian Kagayakiman. Why comedy? People notice the moment things tilt slightly off-center — and laugh. Laughter is a small noticing, a small transformation. A different entrance to the same place as meditation and coaching.

Near-death experience (note)
III

After seeing emptiness, the place I returned to was the everyday. I want to protect the ordinary for the next generation. Everything I do now sits on that axis.

My wife and I run ao. inc., which operates 11 beauty salons. No matter how far AI advances, the experience of being cared for by another human can't be replaced. Facing the person in front of you, with the hospitality that makes them more beautiful — as technology accelerates, that value only grows. That's why, in the age of AI, beauty salons are a growth industry, not a declining one. We're building a hospitality-driven business with our hands, together, as a family.
